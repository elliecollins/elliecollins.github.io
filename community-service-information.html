Community Service Index

BRACE teaches volunteers how to assist their communities in the case of an emergency. By volunteering with BRACE, students can gain vital hands-on skills in disaster preparedness, basic first aid, fire safety, and light search and rescue.

There are two ways to volunteer with BRACE:

  • Community Emergency Response Team (CERT): Click this link to apply.
  • Pensacola Fire Corps: Click this link to apply.

Bracelets to Backpacks helps provide essentials and encouragement to students and families in need through service-driven initiatives.

The Children's Home Society of Florida provides care and resources for children facing poverty and coming out of traumatic situations.

Students can assist this society by purchasing and donating backpacks, along with a minimum of 5 other items from one of these wishlists. Donations can be dropped off at 17 S. De Villiers Street any time from 8am to 5pm on Tuesdays and Thursdays through the month of August. Each kit is worth 2 service hours, and there is no maximum to how much you can donate.

Important Steps to Receive Volunteer Hours:

  1. Create a profile: chsfl.volunteerhub.com
  2. Register under: “Back to School Kits”
  3. Once completed, record your donation: https://chsfl.org/chs-gifts/

Access all of this information at this link.

Escambia County Animal Welfare cares for abandoned animals through foster programs and the local animal shelter. Students can gain volunteer hours through spending time at the shelter, located at 200 W. Fairfield Drive; however, it is important to note that fostering animals is not counted as community service.

Volunteers must fill out this form to apply. Once completed, email forms to as_volunteerinfo@myescambia.com.

  • Young adults ages 15-17 can volunteer at the shelter without the supervision of an adult (the staff may request that the young adult have to work with a parent or legal guardian in certain situations)
  • Youth between ages 12-15 are allowed to volunteer under the supervision of a parent or legal guardian who has also been approved as a volunteer at the shelter

More information can be found at this link.

The Florida Public Archaeology Network offers educational and community-service opportunities connected to local history and preservation.

Gulf Coast Culture Fest brings together community events and service opportunities centered on culture, connection, and local celebration.

Gulf Coast Diplomacy Youth Diplomats supports youth leadership, civic engagement, and service through diplomacy-oriented projects.

Key Club offers student-led volunteer service opportunities through school clubs and community-focused projects.

Keep Pensacola Beautiful organizes cleanups and environmental projects that help improve parks, beaches, and neighborhoods across the community.

The City of Pensacola, Parks and Recreation partners with Ocean Hour to host monthly cleanups around the city to help keep Pensacola clean. Students can volunteer at various cleanups throughout the month. Each cleanup is about an hour long.

All volunteers must fill out this form.

  • Chimney Park Cleanups: 2nd Saturday of every month (Sign-in from 7:45am to 8:45; lasts about an hour)
  • Bartram Park, Graffiti Bridge, Veteran’s Memorial Park, and Wayside Park Project/Greenshores Cleanups: 3rd Saturday of each month

Ocean Hour brings students together for beach and marine conservation activities aimed at protecting coastal environments.

Open Books Pensacola helps provide books and literacy resources to local families and students. Volunteers may assist with sorting donations, organizing events, or supporting reading programs.

Pensacola MESS Hall connects volunteers with food-focused service projects that support local meals, community outreach, and neighborhood care.
