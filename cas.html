CAS

IB CAS stands for Creativity, Activity, and Service. It is a required part of the International Baccalaureate experience and encourages students to grow through meaningful, real-world experiences.

What CAS requires

  • Creativity: activities that involve imagination, expression, or original ideas.
  • Activity: physical movement, challenge, or personal fitness-based experiences.
  • Service: helping others or contributing to the community in a thoughtful and sustained way.

Key expectations

  • Students must complete a balanced range of experiences across all three areas.
  • CAS is typically completed over an extended period, usually the full duration of the IB Diploma Programme.
  • Students should take part in activities that are personal, meaningful, and challenging.
  • A CAS project is required and should involve planning, initiative, and collaboration.

Learning outcomes

Students are expected to show growth in the following areas:

  • Increased awareness of personal strengths and areas for growth
  • Undertaking new challenges
  • Planning and initiating activities
  • Working collaboratively with others
  • Showing perseverance and commitment
  • Engaging with issues of global importance
  • Considering ethical implications

Reflection and evidence

Students are also expected to reflect on their experiences and keep evidence of their participation. Reflection helps connect the activity to personal development and the IB learner profile.
